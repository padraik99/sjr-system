Ligament Biology · Conservative Management · Dietz Sequencing

The ACL — Biology & Approach

Both ACL ends are intact. This is not a rupture — it is a sprain with structural continuity. The remodeling protocol drives collagen cross-linking via progressive mechanical load (Baar: collagen window 15g hydrolyzed + Vit C, 30–60 min pre-loading). Dietz triphasic sequencing governs phase architecture: eccentric loading first (Nordic eccentrics, slow RDL descent) → isometric integration (wall sit, Spanish squat, canal rock holds) → concentric expression (trap bar heavy, sled forward drag, bilateral then unilateral plyo). This is not an arbitrary ordering — connective tissue adapts in this sequence.

Filbay & Grindem 2019: conservative management produces equivalent outcomes to ACL reconstruction in athletes with intact tissue, good neuromuscular function, and coper status. Her hop data and clinical presentation confirm this profile. Bosch: the Biodex shows strength is symmetric — the problem is coordination speed under unpredictable load. The canal perturbation work and curved run intervals are the primary NMC intervention, not a supplement.

Right Leg · Kicking Foot + Primary Plant Foot

Dual Exposure — Sequencing Logic

Most ACL programs manage one exposure profile. Yari has two: the right leg is both the kicking foot (high-velocity hip flexion, hamstring deceleration at follow-through) and the primary plant foot (single-leg deceleration + rotation under load — the ACL injury mechanism). These are introduced sequentially, not simultaneously.

⚠ Inside Hook Turn — Last In

Receive → plant right → rotate left over planted leg. Combined flexion, valgus, and internal tibial rotation under load. The classic ACL re-injury mechanism. This movement is not in the current program. It enters Phase 3 Session C (Saturday field sessions) only after the May gate hop test re-run and bilateral mechanics are confirmed clean under fatigue. No exceptions for timeline pressure.

Current phase (Phase 2–3 transition): straight-line running confirmed, curved runs entering, bilateral plyos this week, single-leg hop-and-stick next. Kicking (straight approach only) enters Session C field sessions when forward running is fully established. Angled approach — which loads the plant leg in rotation — follows the May gate clearance.

Female-Specific Physiology · Baar / Lee / Hewett

Estrogen, LOX & Ligament Risk

Estrogen suppresses lysyl oxidase (LOX) — the enzyme responsible for collagen cross-linking in ligament. Lee et al. 2015: peak estrogen around ovulation (days 10–14) suppresses LOX by 60–77%, reducing ligament stiffness. Hewett et al. 2007: ACL injury risk is 4–5× higher in the peri-ovulatory window. For an athlete in active ACL return-to-sport training, this is a session-planning variable.

Exercise Library

Protocol — Four Tracks

Track A daily. Track B joint protection and isometrics. Track C strength and sled. Track D ball reintroduction. Nothing in Track D until Track B and C criteria are met at each phase gate.

A

ROM Restoration + Daily Layer 1

Every day before training and on rest days. ~18 min. Grey / Ward: sensory quality before mechanical load.

Why

9.1° Biodex flexion deficit — the primary clinical flag. Supine, slide heel toward glute using hamstring. 2s pause at end range. Progress marker: right heel matches left in prone hang.

Dose

3×15 right leg · 2s hold · daily

Cue

“Pull the heel in — not down. You're bending the knee, not pressing into the floor.”

Why

Sustained passive flexion load via gravity. Targets posterior capsule stiffness — common contributor to knee flexion deficits. More effective than short repetitions for capsular restriction.

Dose

2×2 min right leg · off bench edge · 1–2 lb ankle weight optional

Cue

“Let the weight do the work. Breathe into the stretch. Don't fight it.”

Why

Targets the 20% NMC acceleration lag directly. Eyes closed removes visual compensation, forces somatosensory input from the joint. Barr: sensory quality at the foot-ankle-knee chain governs everything above it. Canal terrain version (eyes open, uneven surface) is a natural complement.

Dose

3×45s each leg · daily

Cue

“Soft knee. Find stillness from the ankle up. Let the small corrections happen — don't brace.”

B

Joint Protection + Isometrics + NMC

Strength session foundation + canal session core. Rio isometric analgesia. Dietz: eccentric → isometric before concentric loading.

Why

Rio et al. 2015: isometric contractions at 70% MVC suppress cortical inhibition and reduce knee pain by >40% for up to 45 minutes. Run before every strength session — removes the pain signal that would degrade movement quality. Also central to canal sessions where the hold is the training stimulus.

Dose

Canal sessions: 3×45s · Strength sessions: 3×30s at session end · 70° knee angle

Cue

“Even weight through both feet. Push the floor away — think extension, not just holding.”

Why

Petersen et al. 2011: 51% hamstring injury reduction. The hamstring is the primary muscular restraint against anterior tibial translation — the same movement the ACL resists. Agonist/antag ratio below 76% goal on both legs — this is bilateral. Feet anchored under barbell on floor. Band assist from pull-up bar reducing each week.

Dose

Phase 2: 3×4–5 · 5s descent · band assist · both legs · Phase 3: reduce assist progressively

Cue

“Five seconds down — count it. The descent is the work.”

Why

Directly targets the 20% Biodex acceleration time lag. Unpredictable band pulls at hip level force rapid motor unit recruitment. Bosch: coordination must be trained under surprise conditions. Run at end of canal sessions when mildly fatigued. She is solid under perturbation — increase unpredictability, not just volume.

Dose

3×8–10 each leg · random direction + timing · 60s rest between legs

Cue

“React — don't anticipate. Let the pull happen first, then find the ground.”

C

Strength + Sled Complex

Mon/Wed/Fri garage sessions + Thu canal sled. Trap bar, hip thrust, Nordics, single-leg RDL. Sled: backward Phase 1+, lateral Phase 2+, forward Phase 3+.

Why

Primary mass rebuild tool. High handles used in Phase 1 to reduce anterior knee moment. Standard handles Phase 2 onward with progressive overload. McGill: trap bar's centered load is the safest bilateral hip hinge for athletes with active joint issues.

Dose

Phase 2: 4×5 · RPE 7–8 · +5% ceiling per session · Phase 3: 4×4–5 · RPE 8

Cue

“Push the floor away — don't think pull. Hips and shoulders rise together.”

Why

Uthoff: backward dragging eliminates anterior tibial shear force — the exact force that strains the ACL. Athlete faces the sled, walks backward. High quad NMC stimulus. Available immediately without sled modification. Best early aerobic + NMC combination in the inventory.

Dose

5×25–30m · moderate load · Thu canal sessions + Tue as finisher

Cue

“Push the ground away behind you. Stay tall. Drive through each step.”

Why

Ward: frontal plane loading quality governs sagittal efficiency. Dynamic hip abductor loading that significantly outperforms clamshells. Strong hip abductors reduce knee valgus during single-leg landing — the primary mechanical ACL protection pattern.

Dose

3×15m each direction · moderate-heavy · Thu canal sessions

Cue

“Lead with the hip — not the foot. Drive each step, don't shuffle.”

Why

Athlete faces away from sled, straps at waist, drives forward while sled trails at ~10–15% BW. Hip extension dominant — mechanically distinct from forward push (not available). Run before sprint work as resisted acceleration primer. Classic speed-strength tool for attacking midfielders.

Dose

Phase 3: 4×20m · ~10–15% BW · 90s recovery · before run intervals

Cue

“Lean forward into the straps. Drive the knee up, push the ground back.”

Why

Combines hamstring eccentric with single-leg balance and hip hinge — directly replicates the right-leg plant for kicking. Ipsilateral DB easier (Phase 2). Contralateral DB harder and more soccer-specific (Phase 2 end → Phase 3).

Dose

3×8 each leg · 3s eccentric · progress from ipsilateral to contralateral DB

Cue

“Hinge at the hip — not the waist. The back leg goes back without the hip opening.”

D

Bilateral Plyo → Unilateral → Ball

Saturday field sessions. Bilateral plyos weeks 1–2. Unilateral progressive from Week 2. Ball reintroduction Session C when forward running fully established.

Why

Dietz: concentric expression follows eccentric and isometric loading. Bilateral plyos first — two legs absorbing load before single-leg. Landing quality is the training variable, not height or distance. Stick every landing for 2s. Evaluate mechanics: quiet, symmetric, no knee dive.

Dose

3×8 · land soft · 2s stick · Week 1–2 Saturday sessions

Cue

“Land quiet. Stick it. If the knees dive in, reset and redo it.”

Why

First unilateral plyometric exposure. Grindem: >95% LSI required for return to cutting sport — these hops generate the data. Stick 2s every rep. Trainer evaluates right vs left mechanics — any asymmetry in landing position, time to stabilize, or confidence is training data. Compare fresh vs fatigued at the May gate.

Dose

3×5 each leg · forward and lateral · 2s stick · Week 2+ Saturday sessions

Cue

“Land quiet. Stick it — 2 full seconds. If the right lands differently than the left, that's the work.”

Blood Flow Restriction · Occlusion Cuffs · Mass Rebuild

BFR Protocol

BFR generates a hypertrophic stimulus at 20–30% 1RM loads by creating lactate accumulation and cell swelling that drives anabolic signaling (Hughes et al. 2017: equivalent hypertrophy to 70–80% 1RM loading). For Yari: light loads mean low anterior knee stress. Critical for reversing the mass deficit on days when the knee is reactive, and as a supplement to heavy loading on green days.

Prescription

Cuffs at upper thigh · 60–80% limb occlusion pressure · 1×30 / 3×15 at 20–30% 1RM · 30s rest between sets. Amber knee days: replaces loaded squat. Supplement use: run after main strength work. Active recovery: BFR canal walk. Safety: numbness or cold limb = cuff pressure too high — remove immediately.

Structural Inputs · Collagen Window · Mass Rebuild

Nutrition Protocol

Mountjoy et al. 2018: low energy availability independently impairs tissue repair regardless of protein intake. She needs to eat above appetite signals during this rebuild phase. The collagen window is the highest-leverage daily intervention — every session, every time.

⚠ Avoid NSAIDs During Program

Ibuprofen blocks COX-2 — the pathway that initiates both inflammation resolution and collagen synthesis. Baar: NSAIDs suppress the exact adaptation the collagen protocol produces. Wall sit (Rio isometric protocol) is the in-session pain management tool, not ibuprofen.

Psychological Readiness · Ardern et al. 2013 · Threshold ≥65

ACL-RSI Assessment

Ardern et al. 2016: athletes who returned before psychological readiness threshold had significantly higher re-injury rates, independent of physical test results. Score = (sum of 12 items ÷ 120) × 100. Threshold ≥65. Administer at program start (baseline), May gate, June gate. Items 1–4 and 9–12 are reverse-scored.

Scoring
Score = (sum ÷ 120) × 100 = %. Reverse-score items 1–4, 9–12: a score of 8 becomes 2, a 3 becomes 7. Low Emotion + high Confidence = best psychological return profile.
