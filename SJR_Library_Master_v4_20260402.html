Nordic Hamstring Curl
Petersen et al. 2011: 51% hamstring injury reduction — but the research pool is overwhelmingly male (13 of 15 studies). Male athletes: non-negotiable. Knee-dominant eccentric directly addresses the distal injury mechanism dominant in male sprinters. Use band-assisted entry. Female athletes (Baar / Bosch): anterior pelvic tilt in female sprinters loads the proximal hamstring via a hip-dominant mechanism the Nordic does not address. Bosch: the hamstring must function isometrically across both joints at top speed — isolated knee-dominant eccentric is insufficient alone. Prescription for female sprint athletes: Nordic as part of a paired program only — always combined with single-leg RDL, GHR, or single-leg Roman Chair to address the proximal, hip-dominant end. Never as a standalone intervention.
Petersen · Baar · Bosch

Single-Leg Roman Chair (Hip-Dominant Hamstring)
Bosch's preferred hamstring loading tool for sprint athletes — and the specific prescription for female sprinters as a Nordic curl complement. Meets four criteria simultaneously: sufficient single-leg overload; hamstring attempts to extend the hip AND prevent knee extension; load occurs at optimal muscle length; pelvis can rotate freely to find that optimal length. Trains the isometric coordination function the hamstring must perform at top-end sprint speed — not just eccentric strength at the knee. For female sprint athletes with anterior pelvic tilt, this addresses the proximal hip-dominant injury mechanism the Nordic curl cannot reach.
